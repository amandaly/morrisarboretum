16th Annual Woody Plant Conference at Swarthmore College

Friday, July 17, 2015
Lang Performing Arts Center, Swarthmore, PA

Mark your calendars for the 18th annual Woody Plant Conference at Swarthmore College. This popular day-long conference focuses on great woody plants for the Mid-Atlantic states and how to use them in the landscape.

Featured speakers include:

  • Jim Chatfield, Professor, Ohio State University
    Jim will focus on the importance of the planting process and the site in understanding plant health care issues and determining management strategies.
  • David Creech, Professor Emeritus, Stephen F. Austin State University
    Dr. Creech will consider new plants coming on the market and how to select environmentally sensible plants in our landscapes.
  • Patrick Cullina, Horticulture Consultant and former Vice President of Horticulture at the High Line
    Patrick will examine the issues involved with incorporating green infrastructure into public and private landscapes.
  • Rebecca McMackin, Director of Horticulture, Brooklyn Bridge Park
    This talk will focus on the ecological strategies used at the park as well as management plans for encouraging sustainable landscapes.
  • Pandora Young, Senior Gardener, Longwood Gardens
    Young will highlight underused trees, shrubs, and vines that not only have superior beauty but are also edible.

For a complete brochure click here.

This annual conference is co-sponsored by Morris Arboretum, Chanticleer, Longwood Gardens, Pennsylvania Horticultural Society, Scott Arboretum of Swarthmore College, and Tyler Arboretum.

The conference is geared to landscape professionals and avid amateur gardeners.

Continuing education units (CEUs) for PA and NJ landscape architects, International Society of Arboriculture certified arborists, and PA certified horticulturists will be available.
