2015 STEAMpunk Expo

Sunday, June 21 | 10:00am - 8:00pm
Various events throughout the garden.

Explore the “what if?” around every bend as you stroll the dynamic Victorian garden. Discover the alternative aesthetic of steampunk, where a reimagined past creates a fantastic future. Before electricity and fossil fuels, steam was the power that drove adventure and exploration. Come to the Morris Arboretum for a day of events, fashion, food and fun. Watch the sun dip beyond the horizon as we celebrate the longest day of the year with an outdoor evening concert. Purchase tickets through TicketLeap.


Dining Options at the STEAMpunk Expo:

  • Compton Café will be open until 5:00pm
  • Picnicking on the grounds permitted after 5:00pm

Activities include:

Power it Up!

1:00-4:00pm, various locations throughout garden

Imagine if you had to travel by hot air balloon or steam locomotive. Imagine if you had to wind your cell phone to charge it up. Explore the power of the past through the world of STEAM - Science, Technology, Engineering, Art and Math. At activity stations throughout the Arboretum you can experience the fun as you experiment with water power, create a nature sun print, build a wind-up Lego® race car, and construct a self-powered butterfly. Free with regular garden admission, however some activities may have a small fee.

The Museum of Interesting Things

1:00-4:00pm, Visitor Center Lower Gallery

Stop by the Lower Gallery of the Visitor Center to get your hands on the past. The Museum of Interesting Things, a traveling interactive demonstration/exhibition of antiques and inventions will be on hand to inspire your innovation and creativity. Learn from the past to invent a better future. Free with regular garden admission.

Eureka! The Invention Show

1:00-4:00pm, Visitor Center Classroom

Denny Daniel of The Museum of Interesting Things expounds on the Industrial Revolution, the mechanical era, and the earlier forms of green energy with interactive demonstrations of such items as the steam engine, Thomas Edison cylinder phonograph, windup toys and mechanical inventions. *Requires Expo ticket

Steampunk Storytelling

11:30am-12:00pm, Azalea Meadow Stage

Historian, author and professor of arms Mark Donnelly will regale the audience with a tale of steampunk adventure. Suitable for all ages. Free with regular garden admission.

The Language of Flowers*

12:00pm and 2:00pm, Visitor Center Classroom

A flower is worth more than a thousand words! The tender loveliness of the essence of flowers can cast a magical spell and bring joy to the heart. This presentation, by Priscilla Gene W. Shaffer, instructor of flower arranging at Temple University/Ambler, and owner of Arts & Flowers, s'il vous plait! Inc., will include images of these gifts of nature, as well as discuss the symbolism attributed to these natural beauties through the ages. *Requires Expo ticket

Grist Mill Demonstration

1:00 - 4:00pm, Takes place at Bloomfield

Visit historic Springfield Mills at Morris Arboretum’s Bloomfield Farm. At the mill, which was once powered by water, enjoy watching the corn ground and baked into muffins. Free with regular garden admission.

Contraptions – The Rube Goldberg Variations*

1:00pm - 2:00pm, Upper Gallery

Historian and author Mark Donnelly presents the inventions no one mentions. Professor Donnelly’s research into science gone wrong will captivate and amuse you. *Requires Expo ticket.

Pterodomania – The Victorian Craze for Ferns*

2:30-3:30pm, Visitor Center Upper Gallery

Victorians were obsessive collectors, and with the introduction of quicker steamships to transport plants, and the Wardian case to protect the plants as they traveled across oceans, collecting ferns became a Victorian passion. Morris Arboretum’s own plant propagation goddess and delightful speaker, Shelly Dillard, will transport you back with words and images to discover this little known facet of Victorian culture. *Requires Expo ticket.

Fashion Show

Come be awed by the original steampunk creations designed by students from The Art Institute of Philadelphia. After a fashion show on the Azalea Meadow stage, students and their models will be available for up close looks and discussion. Free with regular garden admission.

Archaeology of the Ancient Near East in the Steam and Diesel Eras

4:00-5:00pm, Visitor Center Upper Gallery

In the late 19th century archaeologists began to explore areas of the near east that were little known to Europeans. Hear University of Pennsylvania archaeologist and Steampunk fiction author Brad Hafford discuss the mysteries uncovered in these early expeditions.*Requires Expo ticket.
